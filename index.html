Autobiography (EN)

I am Kevin Lee, a Software Development Engineer in Test currently working in the Sygna team at CoolBitX. I have around four years of experience in software testing, focusing on product function testing, system integration testing, regression testing configuration, and performance testing. I also design and implement automated testing workflows for backend services and frontend applications.

During my bachelor and master studies, I mainly worked on topics related to physiological signals, spectrum analysis, and IoT applications. My undergraduate project designed a leg exercise system controlled by physiological information, dynamically adjusting resistance and motion range based on EMG and temperature signals. My master thesis combined mobile spectral analysis and fluorescent proteins to detect toxic compounds in food, using Python for spectrum analysis and IoTtalk to stream data to backend services for further evaluation.

I also worked as a contract staff member at the Office of Academic Affairs of National Cheng Kung University, where I supported administrative operations and internal systems. In that role, I used Python and Selenium to automate the online document approval workflow, turning a manual process into an automated one and significantly improving efficiency for colleagues.

At CoolBitX, I have led or contributed to multiple testing-related projects, including building release regression testing processes, developing E2E automation for frontend using Selenium, creating API integration test projects with PyTest and Jest, and setting up performance and load tests with k6 on Sygna Bridge and Sygna Hub services. These projects helped reduce overall test time, reveal performance bottlenecks, and provide reliable quality signals for releases.

Going forward, I aim to deepen my skills as an SDET, continue improving in test automation and performance testing, and explore how AI can be applied to testing workflows, such as generating test cases, detecting anomalies, and analyzing results. My goal is to help teams deliver more reliable products with higher efficiency and better visibility into system quality.
